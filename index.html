    Understanding Astrology

    Astrology is the key to predicting a person's past, present and future by observing the alignment of various planets and stars at the time of birth. It is basically a study of human life based on the position of heavenly bodies, i.e. the planets, Sun, Moon and different constellations or sets of stars.

    The zodiac is the belt along which all heavenly bodies travel and impact humans born on different dates and months. Other than that, astrology also has the concept of houses that signify different aspects of an individual’s life, such as career, finance, love, luck, etc.

    All these aspects are combined and in relation to each other to gather the best possible holistic information about an individual and make exact future predictions about them. Unlike what many believe, this is not a random jumble of things. The actual prediction process requires extensive mathematical calculations to make accurate astrology predictions.

    The time, date and place of birth are significant in astrology, as it is necessary to know the interplanetary and constellation positions that will influence the person's life. That's why astrologers make astrology predictions by date of birth.

    Indian astrology has a long history, dating back to centuries ago. We at InstaAstro bring you a platform where you can extensively utilise astrology to handle daily-life situations, challenges and curiosity. Connect to the best astrologer in world today!

    Horoscope In Astrology

    The different concepts of Indian astrology that are studied to form information about a person’s life situations are presented in an astrological chart called “horoscope”. Let us learn about various insights we can receive in a horoscope.

    • Firstly, different planets and their combinations are studied to know the other aspects of an individual's life. The Navgrahas, or nine planets in astrology, are Surya (Sun), Chandra (Moon), Mangal (Mars), Budha (Mercury), Brihaspati (Jupiter), Shukra (Venus), Shani (Saturn), Rahu (Moon's north node) and Ketu (Moon's south node).
    • According to Indian astrology, there are also 12 houses, each focusing on a particular aspect of a person's life. The position of planets in these houses is equally essential to determine that person's luck and life path.
    • When present within a house, the planets associated with positive effects indicate a good time in that aspect of life. For example, the planets associated with positive effects include Venus and Jupiter. On the other hand, the planets typically related to the adverse effects are Rahu, Ketu, Shani, etc.
    • Apart from the Navagrahas, there are Nakshatras - the different constellations in Indian astrology. There are 27 Nakshatras, each of which is subdivided into 4 Padas.
    • Nakshatras and Padas are used for a more in-depth study of the person's subconscious behaviour. For example, the Nakshatras can tell about a person's Guna (the balance of three elemental types of energies- Rajas, Tamas and Sattva), Gana (temperament), prospects in career, marriage, health and so on.
